I. Statement of Purpose and Scope
The primary objective of this collaboration is the optimization of Near-Infrared (NIR) spectral responsivity in silicon bottom cells, specifically utilizing the proprietary Granas-TOPCon architecture. As the thermodynamic limits of single-junction silicon solar cells are approached, the transition to perovskite/silicon tandem configurations is necessitated. This agreement focuses on the fundamental re-engineering of the silicon substrate to act as a dedicated infrared converter within the 800–1200 nm regime.

II. Technical Objectives and Theoretical Framework
The parties shall collaborate to refine the theoretical framework governing the optical and recombination dynamics of the rear contact stack. The research will be guided by the Drude model, which predicts that absorption cross-sections for free carriers increase quadratically with wavelength, necessitating a reduction in either doping concentration ($N$) or physical layer thickness ($d_{poly}$).
+3

To achieve these objectives, the Laboratory and the Participant will utilize the following governing equations to model carrier lifetime and absorption:

$$\frac{1}{\tau_{eff}} = \frac{1}{\tau_{int}} + \frac{J_{0s}(N_A + \Delta n)}{q n_i^2 W}$$
This formula allows the researchers to describe the effective minority carrier lifetime ($\tau_{eff}$) by accounting for intrinsic lifetime ($\tau_{int}$) and surface saturation current density ($J_{0s}$). Furthermore, the collaboration will seek to maximize the path length enhancement factor ($Z$) to approach the Yablonovitch limit:

$$Z = \frac{\int_0^W I(z) dz}{I_0 W (1 - R_f)}$$
Where $I(z)$ represents the intensity distribution and $R_f$ is the front reflectance.

III. Experimental Design: The Granas-TOPCon Architecture
The Participant shall provide the specific architectural specifications for the Granas-TOPCon cell, which deviates from standard industrial "planar rear" designs. The collaboration will focus on the Bifacial-Optimized Rear Contact (BORC) configuration. Key technical specifications include:
+1
 
	•	Substrate: n-type Czochralski (Cz) wafer, 140 $\mu m$ thickness, resistivity 1–3 $\Omega\cdot cm$.  
	•	Rear Interface: Replacement of 150 nm LPCVD polysilicon with a 30 nm ultra-thin nanocrystalline silicon (nc-Si) layer.  
	•	Passivation: Implementation of a hydrogen-rich aluminum oxide ($AlO_x$) capping layer to act as a hydrogen reservoir.  
	•	Optical Buffer: Integration of a low-index dielectric buffer ($MgF_2$ or porous $SiO_2$) before the silver rear reflector to induce Total Internal Reflection (TIR).
