This Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA) establishes the framework for a high-level technical partnership between PRIMEnergeia Granas and the Advanced Materials Research Laboratory (AMRL). This agreement facilitates the rigorous empirical validation and advanced micromechanical characterization of the Torayca® T700S 12K / Toray 2500 Epoxy System. The primary objective is to authenticate the theoretical performance of this specific Unidirectional (UD) Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer (CFRP) configuration for high-compression, weight-sensitive structural applications.
+3


I. Statement of Technical Purpose
The collaboration shall focus on the precision quantification of the synergistic interactions between the Torayca® T700S high-tensile strength intermediate modulus fiber and the Toray 2500 structural epoxy matrix. While preliminary analysis suggests a laminate density of ~1.56 g/cm³ and a compressive strength of ≈ 1.47 GPa, this CRADA authorizes the transition from theoretical modeling to high-fidelity laboratory simulation. The parties shall investigate the matrix's efficacy in stabilizing fibers against micro-buckling modes, which is the governing factor in achieving the reported compressive performance.
+4

II. Technical Specifications and Performance Thresholds
The research conducted under this agreement must adhere to the rigorous material benchmarks previously identified. The T700S 12K reinforcement provides a tensile strength of 4900 MPa and a modulus of 230 GPa. The Toray 2500 resin system, characterized by a 120 °C cure cycle, must be processed to ensure a normalized Fiber Volume Fraction ($V_f$) of exactly 60%. Success is defined by the material's ability to maintain a density below 1.6 g/cm³ while sustaining a compressive load exceeding the 1.2 GPa threshold required for PRIMEnergeia Granas structural applications.
+4

The theoretical density ($\rho_c$) shall be governed by the Rule of Mixtures, expressed as:

$$\rho_c = V_f \rho_f + V_m \rho_m$$
In this formulation, $\rho_f$ is the fiber density (1.80 g/cm³), $V_m$ is the matrix volume fraction (0.40), and $\rho_m$ represents the matrix density, estimated at approximately 1.20 g/cm³.

III. Scope of Work and Methodology
The parties agree to execute a multi-phase investigative protocol designed to eliminate the discrepancies between datasheet values and manufactured component performance. The laboratory simulation shall prioritize:
 
	•	Micromechanical Validation: Utilizing advanced imaging to confirm the interfacial shear strength and fiber alignment within the Toray 2500 matrix.  
	•	Compression Loading Simulation: Implementing physical testing protocols, specifically ASTM D6641 (Combined Loading Compression), to validate the reported 1.47 GPa compressive strength. +1  
	•	Density and Porosity Analysis: Verifying that the final laminate configuration does not exceed the 1.56 g/cm³ calculated density, ensuring the material retains its superior specific strength ratio.  
IV. Laboratory Test Simulation and Results
As part of this agreement, a controlled simulation was performed to assess the material’s response to progressive axial compression. A $10 \text{ mm} \times 10 \text{ mm} \times 2 \text{ mm}$ UD laminate specimen was modeled under a quasi-static loading environment.
