## 1. Purpose

This document defines how PRIMEnergeia measures, reports, and verifies the economic savings ("capital rescued") generated by its HJB-optimal dispatch engine. It is designed to be auditable by independent third parties using publicly available market data.

---

## 2. Recovery Formula

### Definition

Capital Rescued per interval is the incremental revenue generated by PRIMEngine's optimal dispatch versus a defined baseline dispatch strategy, measured at actual settlement prices:

```
ΔR(t) = [ P_prime(t) - P_baseline(t) ] × LMP_RT(t) × Δt

Capital Rescued = Σ ΔR(t)   for all t ∈ [t_start, t_end]
```

Where:
| Symbol | Definition | Units |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| `P_prime(t)` | Power dispatched by PRIMEngine at interval `t` | MW |
| `P_baseline(t)` | Power that would have been dispatched under baseline strategy | MW |
| `LMP_RT(t)` | Real-time settlement price at the node at interval `t` | $/MWh |
| `Δt` | Settlement interval duration | hours |

### Important Clarifications

- **Only incremental revenue counts.** Revenue that the baseline strategy would also have captured is excluded.
- **Degradation costs are subtracted.** Battery degradation cost (Rainflow counting + replacement cost amortization) is deducted from gross rescue.
- **Negative intervals are included.** If PRIMEngine underperforms the baseline in any interval, that negative delta reduces the total.

## 3. Baseline Dispatch Strategy

The baseline is a **Naive Time-of-Use (TOU) schedule** — the simplest reasonable strategy an operator would employ without optimization software:

### ERCOT Baseline

| Hour (CT) | Action | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| 00:00 – 06:00 | CHARGE at max rate | Off-peak valley prices |
| 06:00 – 14:00 | HOLD | Mid-day solar suppression |
| 14:00 – 20:00 | DISCHARGE at max rate | Afternoon/evening peak |
| 20:00 – 00:00 | HOLD | Declining load |

### SEN Baseline

| Hour | Action | Rationale |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 00:00 – 06:00 | CHARGE | Off-peak PML |
| 06:00 – 12:00 | HOLD | Morning ramp |
| 12:00 – 20:00 | DISCHARGE | Peak PML window |
| 20:00 – 00:00 | HOLD | Evening decline |

### MIBEL Baseline

| Hour (CET) | Action | Rationale |
|-------------|--------|-----------|
| 00:00 – 07:00 | CHARGE | OMIE off-peak pool |
| 07:00 – 13:00 | HOLD | Solar suppression |
| 13:00 – 21:00 | DISCHARGE | Evening peak |
| 21:00 – 00:00 | HOLD | Declining demand |

### Why This Baseline is Conservative

- A naive TOU schedule is the **minimum competence standard** — any operator with calendar access could implement it.
- This means PRIMEngine's savings are measured against a reasonable alternative, not against zero dispatch.
- If an operator currently uses a less-optimized strategy than TOU, their actual savings would be **higher** than reported.

## 4. Measurement Protocol

### 4.1 Time Resolution

| Market | Settlement Interval | PRIMEngine Decision Interval |
|--------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| ERCOT | 15 min (RT) / 1 hr (DA) | 15 min |
| SEN | 15 min (CENACE) | 15 min |
| MIBEL | 1 hr (OMIE) | 1 hr |

### 4.2 Price Sources

| Market | Day-Ahead Source | Real-Time Source | Authority |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| **ERCOT** | DAM Settlement Point Prices | RTM Settlement Point Prices (15-min) | ERCOT MIS Public Reports |
| **SEN** | MDA Precios Marginales Locales | MTR Precios Marginales Locales | CENACE SIM Portal |
| **MIBEL** | OMIE Day-Ahead Pool Price | OMIE Intraday Auction Price | OMIE Public Results |

All prices are sourced from the grid operator's public settlement reports, which are independently verifiable.

### 4.3 Settlement Alignment

- PRIMEngine uses the **same settlement clock** as the market operator.
- ERCOT: Interval Ending (IE) convention, CT timezone.
- SEN: Interval Beginning (IB) convention, Mexico City timezone.
- MIBEL: Hourly, CET timezone.

## 7. Independent Verification Procedure

A third party can reproduce PRIMEngine's reported results using this procedure:

### Step 1: Obtain Settlement Data

Download the relevant settlement prices from the market operator's public portal:
- **ERCOT:** [MIS Public Reports](https://mis.ercot.com) → Report ID NP4-190-CD (DAM SPP)
- **SEN:** [CENACE SIM](https://www.cenace.gob.mx/SIM/PML/) → Precios Marginales Locales
- **MIBEL:** [OMIE Results](https://www.omie.es/en/market-results)

### Step 2: Reconstruct Baseline

Apply the baseline TOU schedule (Section 3) to the settlement interval data. Calculate what the revenue would have been under the baseline:

```
R_baseline = Σ [ P_baseline(t) × LMP_RT(t) × Δt ]
```

### Step 3: Compare to PRIMEngine Dispatch Log

Using PRIMEngine's audit trail CSV (Section 5), compute:

```
R_prime = Σ [ P_prime(t) × LMP_RT(t) × Δt ]
```

### Step 4: Verify Capital Rescued

```
Capital Rescued = R_prime - R_baseline
```

This figure should match PRIMEngine's reported Capital Rescued within rounding tolerance (±$1).

## 8. Risk Disclosures

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| **Simulated vs. Real** | All dashboard results are clearly labeled as "🔮 Simulated" or "📂 Historical" via the mode banner. Simulated results are NOT used for fee calculation. |
| **Proxy Data** | When real data is unavailable, proxy data is generated from documented ERCOT price patterns. All proxy data is labeled with `[PROXY DATA]` tags and an orange warning banner. |
| **Backtest ≠ Future** | Past performance does not guarantee future results. Backtest results use historical settlement prices and assume perfect execution. Real-world execution may vary due to ramp rate limits, communication latency, and curtailment orders. |
| **Single Node Reference** | The VZA-400 result ($231,243 projected recoverable) is modeled from publicly available CENACE data at a single node. No proprietary work was performed at this node. Extrapolations to other nodes assume similar grid dynamics, which may not hold. |
| **Battery Degradation Model** | The Rainflow cycle counting model uses manufacturer-typical parameters. Actual degradation varies by cell chemistry, ambient temperature, and operating history. |
