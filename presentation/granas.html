The Problem

Regular solar panels have hit a ceiling.

🔒

Silicon Maxed Out

Standard silicon panels top out around 22-24% efficiency. Physics says one material can only do so much — there's a hard limit called the Shockley-Queisser limit.

🌈

Wasted Sunlight

Silicon is great at catching red light, but it lets a lot of blue and green light go to waste. That's free energy falling on the panel and getting turned into heat instead of electricity.

🎲

Recipe Guessing Games

Developing new solar cell recipes takes hundreds of trial-and-error experiments. Each failed batch wastes expensive chemicals, lab time, and months of progress.

💡 The solution: stack two materials — one for blue/green, one for red/infrared — and let AI find the perfect recipe.
What is Granas™

A tandem solar cell.

Think of it like a double-decker bus for sunlight. The top deck (perovskite) catches the high-energy photons. The bottom deck (silicon TOPCon) catches everything that passes through.

Top: Perovskite Catches blue & green light λ 400–750 nm
ETFE Cover 96% transparent shield
Bottom: Silicon TOPCon Catches red & infrared light λ 750–1200 nm
CFRP Backbone Carbon fiber — strong & light
1

Perovskite Top Cell

A crystal ink (MAPbI₃) is deposited as a thin film. It absorbs the high-energy part of the solar spectrum that silicon normally wastes.

2

Silicon Bottom Cell

A TOPCon (tunnel oxide) silicon cell catches everything the perovskite let through. It's the proven workhorse of the solar industry.

3

Green Albedo Cooling

The panel reflects 535 nm green light back out. This isn't useful for electricity, but it cools the cell, which makes everything else work better.

4

Result: >33% Efficiency

By combining two layers plus smart light management, Granas™ achieves ~33.5% tandem efficiency — far beyond what either layer can do alone.

Physics Inside

Real science. Not guesswork.

Every engine uses real physics equations — the kind you find in peer-reviewed papers. Here's what each one models:

🔬 Light Trapping (Optics)

Mie scattering — models how tiny perovskite granules bend and scatter photons to keep them inside the cell longer. TMM (transfer matrix) calculates reflections at every layer boundary.

🧪 Crystal Growth (SDL)

LaMer nucleation — predicts how perovskite crystals form from solution. Scherrer equation — estimates grain size. Bigger grains = fewer defects = more voltage.

🌿 Cooling (Albedo)

Reflects 535 nm green light back out. The cell runs 8–12°C cooler, which directly boosts open-circuit voltage and extends the panel's lifetime (T80).

🧬 Optimal Baking (HJB)

Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman — the same math from PRIME Grid! Here it computes the perfect temperature ramp for annealing the perovskite film to maximize crystal quality.

Beyond Electricity

Sun → Electricity → Hydrogen → Fertilizer.

Granas doesn't stop at making electricity. Two extra engines take the power output further:

💧

Green Hydrogen (H₂)

A PEM electrolyzer splits water into hydrogen and oxygen using the electricity from Granas panels. The hydrogen is stored and can power fuel cells, trucks, or industrial processes — all from sunlight.

⚗️

Green Fertilizer (GHB)

The Green Haber-Bosch engine takes nitrogen from the air and hydrogen from water, and combines them into ammonia (NH₃) — the building block of fertilizer. No fossil fuels needed.

🌱 One Granas module can produce electricity, hydrogen, and fertilizer — three products from a single panel on your roof.
