A-ICE-G1 Deep Dive

The ammonia engine explained.

1

Why Ammonia?

Ammonia (NH₃) is zero-carbon: it's just nitrogen + hydrogen. It's liquid at mild pressure (8.6 bar), easy to store, and we already ship millions of tons of it globally for fertilizer.

2

The Challenge: Hard to Ignite

Ammonia's flame speed is 7 cm/s — six times slower than gasoline. It needs help. That's where the H₂ pilot comes in: a tiny 8% hydrogen jet that lights the ammonia instantly.

3

Our Software Handles the Hard Part

We compute the combustion model (Wiebe function), manage the dual-fuel injection timing, control NOx with SCR aftertreatment, and run the thermal management — all in software.

4

Result: 335 kW, Zero Carbon

Enough power for a 40-ton long-haul truck. 44% brake thermal efficiency. Euro VI emissions compliant. Zero CO₂. Drop-in replacement for diesel ECU software.

PEM-PB-50 Deep Dive

Hydrogen in, electricity out.

A fuel cell is the opposite of an electrolyzer. Instead of splitting water with electricity, it combines hydrogen + oxygen to produce electricity + water. No combustion. Silent. No vibration.

1

Nernst Equation

Every cell generates ~1.0 V from the thermodynamic potential of the H₂ + O₂ reaction. Stack 370 cells = ~370 V DC bus.

2

Three Kinds of Losses

Activation (catalyst needs energy to start the reaction), Ohmic (membrane resistance), and Mass transport (running out of gas at high current).

3

Balance of Plant

Air compressor, coolant pump, and hydrogen recirculation consume ~10% of gross power. Our software optimizes these to maximize net output.

4

10,000 Hour Durability

We model voltage degradation at 2.5 µV/h — matching DOE 2030 targets. After 10,000 hours, the stack still retains ~97% of its power.

HY-P100 Deep Dive

A tiny turbine. Massive flexibility.

The HY-P100 is a micro gas turbine that spins at 70,000 RPM using hydrogen. It can start cold in 5 minutes, hot in 1 minute, and ramp from idle to full power in 10 seconds.

🌀 Brayton Cycle

Compress air → add H₂ → burn in a lean premixed combustor → expand through a turbine → spin a permanent magnet generator. The exhaust is hot (280°C) — perfect for heat recovery (CHP) reaching 85% total efficiency.

🏭 Use Cases

Grid peaking: start fast, ramp fast, fill in gaps when solar/wind drops. Ship propulsion: compact, lightweight, hydrogen-ready. Remote power: microgrids and mining camps where diesel is the only option today.
